The Complete Guide to Burger King Croissan'wiches

The Croissan'wich is undoubtedly the crown jewel of the Burger King breakfast menu. Invented in 1983 in the United States and later introduced to the UK market, the Croissan'wich has become synonymous with a quality fast-food morning meal. But what exactly makes this breakfast sandwich so special, and how do you choose the right one for your morning?

At its core, every Croissan'wich is built around a warm, flaky croissant bun — the kind that crumbles just slightly when you bite into it, releasing buttery layers of pastry that perfectly complement the savoury fillings within. This is not a hard croissant or a stale one; it's baked fresh and served warm, creating that distinctive soft-yet-flaky texture that makes the Croissan'wich unlike any other breakfast sandwich on the UK high street.

🥚 The Egg – The Heart of Every Croissan'wich

Every Croissan'wich on the Burger King breakfast menu comes with a freshly prepared egg. The egg is cooked to a rounded shape that perfectly fits the croissant bun, creating that satisfying bite in every mouthful. The egg provides a good source of protein and healthy fats, making the Croissan'wich a genuinely filling breakfast option that keeps hunger at bay for hours.

The egg is a crucial flavour component — it binds the other ingredients together and provides a rich, savoury base that elevates everything above it. Whether you choose beef, chicken strips, or the Chick'N Crisp version, the egg is what makes every Croissan'wich taste complete and cohesive.

🧀 The Cheese – Melted Perfection

Sitting between the egg and the protein is a slice of melted American-style cheese. When the Croissan'wich is assembled while hot, the cheese melts into the egg and protein layers, creating a gooey, creamy element that adds richness and depth of flavour. The cheese also helps bind all the layers together, preventing the sandwich from falling apart as you eat it.

🍖 Choosing Your Protein

The Croissan'wich range offers five distinct protein options, each designed to appeal to different taste preferences and dietary considerations:

  • Fully Loaded Croissan'wich with Egg: The premium option — featuring both sausage and bacon alongside the egg and cheese. If you want the most indulgent, satisfying morning sandwich, this is the one to choose. It's the best seller for good reason, packing maximum flavour into every bite.
  • Beef Croissan'wich with Egg: A flame-grilled beef patty with egg and cheese. The beef is cooked using BK's signature flame-grilling method, giving it a slightly charred, smoky flavour that works beautifully with the buttery croissant. A great choice for beef lovers who want a more substantial morning meal.
  • Beefacon Croissan'wich with Egg: This is the best of both worlds — a combination of beef and bacon with egg and cheese. The bacon adds a saltier, crispier contrast to the juicy beef patty, creating a more complex and layered flavour profile. Perfect for those who can't decide between the two.
  • Chicken Strips Croissan'wich with Egg: Crispy chicken strips replace the beef or sausage here. The strips provide a satisfying crunch and a leaner protein source, making this a popular choice for chicken lovers and those looking for something slightly lighter than the beef options.
  • Chick'N Crisp Croissan'wich with Egg: The lightest of the Croissan'wich range, featuring a single crispy Chick'N Crisp fillet with egg and cheese. It delivers great flavour and texture without being as heavy as the other options — ideal if you want something satisfying but not too filling early in the morning.

🌟 Pro Tip: The Fully Loaded Croissan'wich is consistently rated as the best value breakfast item at Burger King UK. With three protein elements (sausage, bacon, and egg) plus cheese on a premium croissant, it offers the most flavour per penny of any breakfast sandwich on the menu.

Why the Croissant Bun Makes All the Difference

Many fast-food breakfast sandwiches use standard round buns or English muffins, but the croissant bun used in the Burger King Croissan'wich is genuinely different. Its high butter content creates a richness that complements savoury fillings in a way that a plain bread bun simply cannot. The layers of the croissant also create textural contrast — some areas are slightly crispy on the outside while remaining soft and almost pastry-like in the middle.

This is why so many people who try a Croissan'wich for the first time are immediately impressed. It feels more premium than a standard fast-food breakfast sandwich, even though it remains perfectly accessible and affordable. The croissant bun elevates the entire breakfast experience in a way that has helped the Croissan'wich range maintain its popularity for decades.

What Makes BK Breakfast Different From the Competition?

Understanding why Burger King breakfast has maintained such a loyal customer base in the UK requires examining what makes it genuinely different from other fast-food breakfast options available on the British high street. There are several key differentiators that consistently emerge in customer feedback and reviews.

1. The Flame-Grilling Difference

The most fundamental distinction between Burger King and virtually every other fast-food breakfast provider in the UK is the flame-grilling technique. While most competitors fry, grill on a flat top, or microwave their breakfast proteins, Burger King uses real flames to cook its beef products. This creates genuine char marks and a smoky flavour that simply cannot be replicated by other cooking methods. Even if you close your eyes, you can taste the difference — and that distinctive quality is one of the biggest reasons why loyal BK customers return again and again.

2. Premium Croissant Buns vs Standard Bread

The decision to use a croissant bun for the Croissan'wich range was a masterstroke of product development. While McDonald's uses McMuffin buns and many cafés use basic bread rolls, the buttery, layered croissant immediately elevates the perceived quality of the breakfast sandwich. Customers consistently report that eating a Croissan'wich feels like a more premium experience than eating competing breakfast sandwiches at similar price points. The croissant's natural richness complements savoury fillings in a uniquely satisfying way.

3. Genuine Variety Across the Breakfast Range

With five distinct Croissan'wich options, the Breakfast King, Potato Bites, and a full range of hot beverages, Burger King breakfast offers genuine variety. You're not limited to one or two options — you can mix and match protein choices, add sides, and choose from a comprehensive drinks menu that includes espresso, Americano, cappuccino, latte, flat white, hot chocolate, and tea. This breadth of choice means that the Burger King breakfast menu caters to individuals, couples, families, and groups of colleagues without anyone having to compromise.

4. Speed and Convenience

Fast food breakfast is fundamentally about speed and convenience, and Burger King delivers on both fronts. With drive-thru options available at many locations, mobile ordering through the BK app, and streamlined in-store ordering via kiosks, getting your Croissan'wich and Potato Bites is faster than ever. For busy commuters who need to eat on the go without sacrificing quality or flavour, this combination of speed and substance is hard to beat.

5. Value for Money

Despite the premium quality of the croissant buns and flame-grilled proteins, Burger King breakfast remains competitively priced within the UK fast-food market. The Croissan'wiches are priced accessibly, and when combined with Potato Bites and a hot drink, the total cost compares very favourably with high-street café alternatives that may not offer the same quality or quantity of food.

Beyond Breakfast: The Complete Burger King Menu

While the Burger King breakfast menu is the primary focus of this guide, it's worth knowing that after breakfast hours end, Burger King switches to its comprehensive full menu — one of the most varied and beloved fast-food menus in the United Kingdom. Whether you're planning a post-morning visit for lunch, an afternoon snack, or an evening meal, here's a quick overview of what the Burger King UK full menu has to offer.

🍔 The WHOPPER® Range

The WHOPPER® is Burger King's most iconic product and has been a staple of the menu since 1957. Available in the UK in multiple configurations — original WHOPPER, Double WHOPPER, and WHOPPER with Bacon and Cheese — it remains one of the best-selling burgers in fast food history. Each WHOPPER features a flame-grilled beef patty, fresh tomato, lettuce, creamy mayonnaise, ketchup, crunchy pickles, and sliced white onion, all served on a toasted sesame seed bun. The beef patty is significantly larger than those used by most competitors, contributing to the sense of genuine satisfaction that comes from eating a WHOPPER.

👑 The Big King Range

The Big King is BK's answer to the double-deck burger genre, featuring two flame-grilled beef patties, a special sauce, and various toppings on a sesame bun. Available in Big King, Big King Royale, and Big King Double Royale configurations, this range caters to customers who want a multi-patty burger experience with BK's signature flame-grilled flavour.

🍗 Chicken Royale Range

The Chicken Royale is BK's flagship chicken burger — a crispy fried chicken fillet with lettuce and mayo on a sesame seed bun. Over the years, this simple formula has expanded into the Double Chicken Royale, Chicken Royale Bacon and Cheese, and Double Chicken Royale Bacon and Cheese variants, all of which build on the original's winning combination of crunch, tenderness, and flavour.

🌱 Plant-Based Options

Recognising the growing demand for plant-based alternatives, Burger King has expanded its UK menu to include the Plant-Based WHOPPER and the Vegan Royale. Both items are designed to deliver a similar eating experience to their meat-based counterparts, using plant-based patties that mimic the texture and flavour of beef and chicken respectively. These items have been particularly well-received by customers following vegetarian and vegan diets, as well as flexitarians who occasionally opt for plant-based meals.

🥩 Premium Wagyu Range

For customers seeking a more indulgent, premium burger experience, Burger King UK has introduced the Wagyu Wellington and The Wagyu — featuring premium Wagyu beef for a more sophisticated flavour profile and luxurious eating experience. These represent the top tier of the BK burger range and are perfect for special occasions or when you want to treat yourself to something truly exceptional.

Frequently Asked Questions – Burger King Breakfast UK

We've compiled answers to the most commonly asked questions about the Burger King breakfast menu in the UK. If you have a question that isn't answered here, please visit our contact page and we'll do our best to help.

What time does Burger King stop serving breakfast in the UK? ▶
Burger King typically stops serving breakfast at 10:30 AM on weekdays and 11:00 AM on weekends. However, this varies significantly by location. Some high-footfall restaurants in city centres or transport hubs may serve breakfast until later, while some smaller or franchised locations may cut off earlier. Always check with your specific restaurant or use the BK UK app to confirm times. The safest approach is to plan to arrive at least 20 minutes before the listed cutoff time.
What is a Croissan'wich at Burger King? ▶
A Croissan'wich is Burger King's signature breakfast sandwich, served in a warm, flaky croissant bun with egg, cheese, and a choice of protein filling (sausage, bacon, beef patty, or chicken). The name combines "croissant" and "sandwich." It was originally developed in the United States in 1983 and has since become one of the most recognised breakfast items at Burger King globally. In the UK, the Croissan'wich range includes five variants: Fully Loaded, Beef, Beefacon, Chicken Strips, and Chick'N Crisp — all served with egg and cheese.
Does Burger King have vegan or vegetarian breakfast options? ▶
Currently, the dedicated Burger King breakfast menu in the UK focuses on items containing egg, cheese, and meat proteins (beef, sausage, bacon, and chicken). The Croissan'wich sandwiches all contain egg and cheese, making them unsuitable for vegans. For vegetarians, the egg-based options without meat proteins may be discussable with staff, but this is subject to individual restaurant capabilities. During the main menu hours, Burger King does offer the Plant-Based WHOPPER and Vegan Royale. It's recommended to speak directly with your local restaurant about vegetarian and vegan breakfast modifications.
How much does the Burger King breakfast cost in the UK? ▶
Burger King breakfast prices in the UK vary by location and may change over time. As a general guide, individual Croissan'wich sandwiches are typically priced between £3.50 – £5.00, with the Fully Loaded variant at the higher end. Potato Bites are usually available for around £1.50 – £2.50, and hot drinks range from approximately £1.50 – £2.80 depending on the type. Breakfast meal deals, when available, offer better value by combining a Croissan'wich, Potato Bites, and a drink at a bundle price. Always check the current menu board at your local restaurant or the BK UK app for the most accurate pricing, as prices are subject to change.
Can I order Burger King breakfast through the app or drive-thru? ▶
Yes! Burger King breakfast is available through multiple ordering channels at most UK locations: (1) In-store ordering at the counter or self-service kiosks, (2) Drive-thru at locations equipped with this service, and (3) The Burger King UK app, which allows you to pre-order and collect your breakfast, often with access to exclusive deals and rewards points. Mobile ordering via the app is particularly useful during busy weekday morning periods when in-store queues can build up. Some third-party delivery platforms may also offer Burger King breakfast delivery, though this varies by location and time of day.
Is Burger King breakfast available all day? ▶
No. Unlike some food service concepts that offer "all-day breakfast," Burger King operates on a dedicated breakfast service window in the morning before transitioning to its main lunch/dinner menu. The Croissan'wiches, Breakfast King, and Potato Bites breakfast items are exclusively available during the designated breakfast hours. Once breakfast service ends, the kitchen switches to the main menu, and breakfast items are no longer available for the rest of the day. This means you genuinely need to plan ahead if Burger King breakfast is on your agenda for the day.
What allergens are in the Burger King breakfast items? ▶
Burger King breakfast items contain several common allergens. The Croissan'wich range contains gluten (from the croissant bun), eggs, milk/dairy (cheese and butter in the croissant), and potentially soya. Meat proteins (beef, chicken, bacon, sausage) may also carry cross-contamination risks for various allergens depending on preparation methods and supplier ingredients. Always consult the official Burger King UK allergen information, available on their website and in-restaurant, before ordering if you have any food allergies or intolerances. Burger King UK is required by law to provide full allergen information, and staff should be able to assist with specific queries.
What is the best Burger King breakfast item to order? ▶
The overwhelming favourite among regular Burger King breakfast customers is the Fully Loaded Croissan'wich with Egg. It combines sausage, bacon, egg, and cheese on a warm, flaky croissant — offering the most flavour variety and the best value for money of any breakfast item on the menu. For first-time visitors, this is the item we recommend without hesitation. If you prefer chicken, the Chicken Strips Croissan'wich is an excellent alternative. And don't forget to add Potato Bites and a Flat White or Latte to complete your breakfast experience properly.

About Burger King UK – A Brief History

Burger King is one of the world's largest fast-food chains, with thousands of restaurants spanning over 100 countries. The brand was founded in 1953 in Jacksonville, Florida, originally operating under the name "Insta-Burger King." The company was restructured and rebranded as "Burger King" in 1954 when franchisees James McLamore and David Edgerton took over operations in Miami, Florida, and introduced the iconic Broiler — a flame-based cooking method that became the backbone of BK's food philosophy.

"Have it your way" — Burger King's iconic slogan, emphasising the brand's commitment to customer choice and customisation, has been a cornerstone of its identity since the 1970s. — Burger King Brand Heritage

Burger King first arrived in the United Kingdom in 1977, initially through a licensing agreement with J Lyons and Co. The brand grew steadily throughout the 1980s and 1990s, establishing a significant presence across England, Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland. Today, Burger King UK operates hundreds of locations, including restaurants in high streets, retail parks, motorway service areas, airports, train stations, and drive-thru formats across the country.

The UK operation of Burger King is particularly notable for its tailored menu adaptations to meet British consumer preferences. While maintaining the global core of the Burger King menu — including the WHOPPER and Croissan'wich — the UK team has introduced market-specific items and deals that resonate with British customers. The addition of items like the Chicken Royale, which has become one of the most popular chicken burgers in the UK market, demonstrates this commitment to local relevance.

Burger King UK is now owned and operated by Restaurant Brands International (RBI), a Canadian multinational fast-food holding company that also owns Tim Hortons and Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen. RBI's backing has provided Burger King UK with the resources to invest in restaurant refurbishments, digital ordering infrastructure, and menu innovation — all of which benefit the morning breakfast service.

The brand has also been at the forefront of sustainability initiatives in the UK fast-food sector, including efforts to reduce single-use plastics, source ingredients from more sustainable suppliers, and reduce food waste across the supply chain. These initiatives have resonated positively with environmentally conscious UK consumers, particularly younger demographics who consider sustainability when making food purchasing decisions.
